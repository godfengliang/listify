## Rule 1: Pure White Background (RGB 255,255,255)

Amazon requires it. No exceptions.

**Technical spec:**
- Background: #FFFFFF (pure white)
- Image must fill 85%+ of the frame
- No shadows on the background
- No watermarks, logos, or text on the main image

✅ **Good:** Product on pure white, well-lit, fills the frame
❌ **Bad:** Product on gray, beige, or "almost white" background
❌ **Bad:** Product with harsh shadows stretching across the background

**Pro tip:** Most "white background" images on Amazon fail the 85% fill requirement. Zoom in. Fill the frame.

## Rule 2: 1000+ Pixels on the Longest Side

Amazon's zoom-on-hover feature only activates at 1000+ pixels.

| Resolution | Zoom Active? | Looks Professional? |
|---|---|---|
| 500×500 | ❌ No zoom | ❌ Blurry on mobile |
| 1000×1000 | ✅ Yes | ✅ Acceptable |
| 2000×2000 | ✅ Yes | ✅✅ Best — sharp on all screens |
| 3000×3000 | ✅ Yes | ✅✅✅ Overkill but future-proof |

**Minimum:** 1000×1000 pixels.
**Recommended:** 2000×2000 pixels.

## Rule 3: Show the Product ACTUALLY Being Used

This is the #1 difference between amateur and professional listings.

### Amateur Image Set:
1. White background front
2. White background back
3. White background side
4. White background... zzz

### Professional Image Set:
1. Main image (white background)
2. **Lifestyle shot** — product in use, real scenario
3. **Detail shot** — close-up of key feature
4. **Size reference** — product next to a common object
5. **Lifestyle shot 2** — different scenario, different user
6. **What's in the box** — all included items
7. **Comparison** — before/after or vs competitor

**Example — Bluetooth Earbuds:**

| Image # | Type | What It Shows |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Main | Earbuds on white |
| 2 | Lifestyle | Person running, earbuds visible |
| 3 | Detail | Close-up of ear tip, showing ergonomic shape |
| 4 | Size | Earbuds in hand, showing compact size |
| 5 | Lifestyle 2 | Person on plane, noise cancellation in action |
| 6 | Package | Charging case, cable, 3 ear tip sizes laid out |
| 7 | Comparison | SoundWave Pro vs generic earbuds — size comparison |

## Rule 4: Label Your Infographics

Text overlays on images dramatically improve understanding — especially on mobile.

**What to put on infographic images:**
- Key dimensions and measurements
- Feature callouts with arrows
- Comparison data (vs competitor)
- Certification badges (if any)

**Rules for text on images:**
- Font size: large enough to read on mobile (20pt+)
- Contrast: dark text on light area, or white text on dark area
- Keep it to 3-5 callouts per image (don't overcrowd)
- Use consistent colors (match your brand)

## Rule 5: Include a Size Reference

"Shoppers can't touch your product. Help them understand its size."

**Methods that work:**
1. **Hand holding product** — most intuitive
2. **Next to common object** — soda can, phone, coin
3. **On a desk/table** — shows real-world footprint
4. **Dimensions overlay** — L × W × H with arrows

✅ **Good:** Fitness watch on a wrist — immediately understand the size
❌ **Bad:** Fitness watch floating on white at 300% zoom — looks massive, customer disappointed

## Rule 6: Show What's in the Box

Reduce returns by setting accurate expectations.

**What to show:**
- All items included in the purchase, laid out neatly
- Each accessory labeled
- Optional: "What's included" vs "What you also need" (not included)

**Example — Resistance Bands Set:**

> [Image: Everything laid out on a clean surface]
>
> **INCLUDED:**
> ✓ 5 resistance bands (10/20/30/40/50 lbs)
> ✓ Carrying bag
> ✓ Workout guide (printed)
> ✓ Door anchor
>
> **NOT INCLUDED:**
> ✗ Yoga mat (sold separately)

This kind of image reduces "I thought it came with X" returns by 30-50%.

## Rule 7: Use All 7 Image Slots

Amazon gives you up to 7 image slots (+ video). Use every single one.

Empty image slots = leaving money on the table.

**The 7-image formula for any product:**

| Slot | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Main (white bg) | Click-through |
| 2 | Lifestyle shot | Show product in use |
| 3 | Feature infographic | Highlight key benefits |
| 4 | Size/scale reference | Set expectations |
| 5 | What's in the box | Reduce returns |
| 6 | Detail close-up | Build quality perception |
| 7 | Comparison or second lifestyle | Overcome objections |

## Video: The 8th Image

Amazon lets you add a product video. Listings with video convert 30-50% higher.

**What your video should include (30-60 seconds):**
- 0-5s: Hook — show the problem or the result
- 5-25s: Product in use — real scenarios
- 25-45s: Key features demonstrated
- 45-55s: What's included
- 55-60s: CTA — "Click Add to Cart"

**Budget approach:** Shoot with an iPhone on a tripod, good lighting, clean background. Modern smartphones shoot in 4K — more than enough for Amazon.

## Photography by Budget

### $0 Budget (DIY)

**Equipment you already have:**
- Smartphone (iPhone 8+ or equivalent)
- Window light (natural light = best light)
- White poster board (for background)
- Free editing app (Snapseed, Lightroom Mobile)

**Setup:**
1. Place product on white poster board near a large window
2. Shoot at mid-day (11am-2pm) for best natural light
3. Use a tripod or stack of books to stabilize
4. Edit: increase brightness, adjust white balance until background is pure white

### $100 Budget

**Add:**
- Lightbox ($30-50 on Amazon) — consistent lighting, easy white background
- Basic tripod with phone mount ($20)
- Reflector ($15) — fill shadows

### $500+ Budget

**Hire a product photographer:**
- Find on Fiverr or local photography studios
- Cost: $50-150 per image (professional)
- Send them your shot list (exactly which images you need)
- Request both edited JPEGs and RAW files

### Pro Tip: Test Your Images

Before committing, run this test:

1. Put your main image next to the top 5 competitors' main images
2. Squint your eyes
3. Does yours stand out? Or blend in?

If it blends in, reshoot it. Your main image needs to be the most clickable one on the page.

## FAQ

### Can I use stock photos?
For the main image: no. Amazon requires the main image to be of the ACTUAL product you're selling. For lifestyle images: stock photos are allowed but not recommended — customers can tell, and it hurts trust.

### What image format should I use?
JPEG is standard. PNG works too but file sizes are larger. TIFF and GIF are not accepted.

### How do I get a pure white background without a lightbox?
Use the free tool Remove.bg to remove backgrounds automatically. Then place the product on a pure white canvas in any image editor.

### Do lifestyle images need white backgrounds?
No. Lifestyle images should show the product in a real environment. Only the main image requires pure white background.
