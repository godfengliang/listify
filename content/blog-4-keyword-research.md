## Introduction

90% of Amazon sales happen on the first page of search results. If your product isn't there, you're invisible.

Keyword research is the #1 factor that determines whether your listing ranks. Here's how to do it right in 2026.

## Step 1: Start with Amazon's Search Bar

Type your product category and note the auto-complete suggestions. These are real searches that real customers are doing right now.

For "wireless earbuds", Amazon suggests:
- wireless earbuds noise cancelling
- wireless earbuds for running
- wireless earbuds with microphone
- wireless earbuds waterproof

These are your seed keywords.

## Step 2: Analyze Competitor Listings

Look at the top 5 results for your main keyword. Note:
- Which keywords appear in their titles?
- What do their bullets emphasize?
- What search terms are they targeting?

## Step 3: Find Long-Tail Keywords

"Wireless earbuds" = 500K searches, impossible competition.
"Wireless earbuds for small ears noise cancelling" = 3K searches, almost no competition.

Long-tail keywords convert at 2-3x higher rates because the buyer knows exactly what they want.

## Step 4: Organize Keywords by Priority

| Priority | Keyword Type | Example | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🥇 Must-have | Primary keyword | "wireless earbuds" | Title + bullets |
| 🥈 Important | Long-tail | "ANC earbuds for running" | Bullets + description |
| 🥉 Nice-to-have | Related terms | "Bluetooth headphones" | Backend search terms |

## Step 5: Place Keywords Strategically

- **Title**: Primary keyword + 1-2 secondary keywords
- **Bullet Points**: Different keyword in each bullet
- **Description**: Natural keyword distribution
- **Backend**: All remaining keywords (up to 250 bytes)
