## Introduction

The Shopify vs Amazon debate is the most common question new e-commerce sellers ask. Here's the honest answer for 2026: **it depends on your goals, and the real winners sell on both.**

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| Factor | Amazon | Shopify |
|---|---|---|
| Startup cost | $39.99/month + inventory | $29/month + theme + inventory |
| Traffic | Built-in (2.5B/month) | You drive it yourself |
| Fees | 15% per sale | ~2.9% payment processing |
| Brand control | Limited | Full control |
| Customer data | Amazon owns it | You own it |
| Competition | Intense | You vs your marketing |
| Time to first sale | Days | Weeks-months |
| Long-term value | Platform-dependent | Your own asset |

## The Case for Starting on Amazon

**Pros:**
- Immediate access to 300M+ active customers
- Built-in trust (people already buy on Amazon)
- FBA handles shipping and returns
- Fast path to first sale

**Cons:**
- 15% fees eat margins
- You don't own customer relationships
- Algorithm changes can tank your business overnight
- Intense competition on every product

## The Case for Starting on Shopify

**Pros:**
- Full brand control and customization
- You own customer data (email list!)
- Lower fees = higher margins
- No algorithm dependency

**Cons:**
- You must drive your own traffic (ads, SEO, social)
- Slower path to first sale
- More technical setup required
- No built-in trust factor

## The Real Winning Strategy: Sell on BOTH

Here's what successful sellers do in 2026:

1. **Amazon for volume** — Use Amazon's built-in traffic to generate sales and reviews
2. **Shopify for brand** — Build your own store for repeat customers and higher margins
3. **Temu for scale** — Access the value-focused buyer segment
4. **TikTok Shop for virality** — Capture impulse buyers with video content
