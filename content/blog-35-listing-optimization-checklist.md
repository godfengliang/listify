## Section 1: Title (10 points)

| # | Check | ✓ | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Title is under 200 characters | ☐ | Over 200 = listing suppression |
| 2 | Brand name is first | ☐ | Builds recognition, Amazon prefers it |
| 3 | Primary keyword in first 50 characters | ☐ | Highest keyword weight position |
| 4 | Contains 3-5 key features with specifics | ☐ | Not "great battery" — "40-hour battery" |
| 5 | No ALL CAPS words | ☐ | Amazon suppresses all-caps listings |
| 6 | No price, promotions, or "Best Seller" claims | ☐ | Listing suppression risk |
| 7 | No special characters (!!!, ★, ♛) | ☐ | Amazon strips or suppresses |
| 8 | Each word capitalized (Title Case) | ☐ | "Wireless Earbuds" not "wireless earbuds" |
| 9 | No keyword stuffing (repeating same word) | ☐ | "Earbuds Wireless Earbuds Bluetooth Earbuds" = bad |
| 10 | Variant info at end (color, size, quantity) | ☐ | Don't waste prime keyword space on variants |

## FAQ

### How often should I run this audit?
Every 30 days for your top products. Rankings change, competitors change, Amazon's algorithm changes. A listing that scored 45 in January might score 35 in June.

### What if I'm not brand registered?
Skip Section 6 (A+ Content). Score out of 46 instead of 50. Then go apply for Brand Registry — it unlocks A+ Content, Brand Analytics, and Sponsored Brands ads.

### My score is good but I'm still not ranking. Why?
This checklist covers ON-PAGE factors. Off-page factors (sales velocity, reviews, PPC) also affect ranking. If your on-page score is 40+ but you're not ranking, the problem is likely sales velocity or competition level.
