## The Short Answer

**FBA wins for most sellers in 2026.** But not always.

FBA = Amazon stores, packs, and ships your products. You pay fees but save time.
FBM = You store, pack, and ship yourself. You save fees but spend time.

The right choice depends on your product, margin, and volume.

FBA wins by $2.20/unit on this product. But let's look deeper.

## When FBA Is Better

✅ **You want to scale** — FBA handles 10 or 10,000 orders without you packing a single box

✅ **Your product is small and light** — FBA fees are weight-based. Light items ($1-3 per fulfillment) are highly profitable on FBA

✅ **You want Prime eligibility** — FBA products get the Prime badge. Prime members spend 2-3× more than non-Prime

✅ **You value your time** — Packing orders yourself = $0/hour. Time spent packing is time NOT spent on product research, listing optimization, or marketing

✅ **Buy Box advantage** — FBA sellers win the Buy Box more often than FBM sellers at the same price

## When FBM Is Better

✅ **Large/heavy products** — FBA fees scale with size. A 20-lb dumbbell set costs $15+ in FBA fees but only $12 to ship yourself

✅ **Slow-moving inventory** — FBA charges long-term storage fees after 365 days. If your product sells 1 unit/month, FBM avoids these fees

✅ **Handmade/custom products** — Products you make to order don't need to sit in Amazon warehouses

✅ **You already have a warehouse** — If you're already shipping orders from your own space, adding FBM is marginal cost

✅ **Testing new products** — FBM for the first 20-50 units to validate demand before sending inventory to FBA

## The Hybrid Approach (What Smart Sellers Do)

**Launch with FBA + backup FBM.**

1. Send 200 units to FBA (your main inventory)
2. Keep 50 units at home for FBM (backup)
3. If FBA runs out of stock, switch to FBM immediately
4. When FBA restocks, switch back

This prevents the nightmare scenario: running out of stock on your best-selling product and losing ranking.

## FBA Fee Calculator (Quick Formula)

```
FBA Fee = $2.50 base + ($0.38 × weight_in_lbs) + ($0.03 × volume_in_cubic_inches)

For small standard (under 1 lb):
FBA Fee ≈ $3.00 - $4.50

For large standard (1-3 lbs):
FBA Fee ≈ $5.00 - $7.50
```

**Rule of thumb:** If FBA fees are less than 25% of your selling price, FBA is profitable.

## The Hidden Costs Nobody Talks About

### FBA Hidden Costs:
- **Returns processing** — FBA handles returns but you still pay return processing fees ($2-5 per return)
- **Removal orders** — Unsellable inventory costs $0.50-1.00 per unit to remove or destroy
- **Storage spikes** — Q4 storage fees are 2-3× higher (October-December)
- **Inbound shipping to Amazon** — You pay to ship products from China → Amazon warehouse

### FBM Hidden Costs:
- **Your time** — Packing 20 orders/day = 2-3 hours/day = $0/hour
- **Customer service** — FBM means you handle all customer messages directly
- **Returns management** — You process, inspect, and repackage returns
- **Late shipments** — One late shipment >4% rate and your account gets suspended

## How to Decide (Decision Tree)

```
Is your product under 3 lbs?
├── YES → Is your margin above 30% with FBA fees?
│   ├── YES → Use FBA ✅
│   └── NO → Can you reduce product cost?
│       ├── YES → Reduce cost, then FBA
│       └── NO → Use FBM until you can
└── NO → Is your product over 5 lbs?
    ├── YES → Use FBM (or Seller Fulfilled Prime)
    └── 3-5 lbs → Compare exact FBA fees vs your shipping cost
```

## FAQ

### Can I switch from FBM to FBA later?
Yes. Convert the listing to FBA in Seller Central, ship inventory to Amazon, and you're switched. Your reviews and ranking stay intact.

### What is Seller Fulfilled Prime?
SFP lets you ship from your own warehouse with the Prime badge — but you must meet strict 2-day delivery requirements. Very hard for new sellers.

### Do I need a business license for FBA?
Yes, Amazon requires business verification for all seller accounts, FBA and FBM.
