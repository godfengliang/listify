## Why Your Amazon Title Is Everything

Your title is the first thing Amazon's algorithm reads. It's also the first thing shoppers read.

**A bad title:**
- Misses key search terms → your product is invisible
- Confuses shoppers → they scroll past
- Gets suppressed by Amazon → your listing disappears

**A good title:**
- Ranks for 10+ keywords on page 1
- Convinces shoppers to click in under 1 second
- Meets all Amazon guidelines → never suppressed

Let's build the good kind.

## The Amazon Title Formula (2026 Update)

```
[Brand Name] + [Core Keyword] + [Key Feature 1] + [Key Feature 2] + 
[Material/Size/Color] + [Use Case/Package Info]
```

**Real example breakdown:**

> **SoundWave Pro Wireless Earbuds — 40-Hour Battery, Active Noise Cancellation, IPX7 Waterproof, Bluetooth 5.3, Deep Bass Stereo Sound, with Wireless Charging Case (Midnight Black)**

| Title Element | Example | Why It's There |
|---|---|---|
| Brand | SoundWave Pro | Brand recognition, trust |
| Core Keyword | Wireless Earbuds | Main search term |
| Feature 1 | 40-Hour Battery | Top selling point |
| Feature 2 | Active Noise Cancellation | Differentiator |
| Feature 3 | IPX7 Waterproof | Use case expansion |
| Feature 4 | Bluetooth 5.3 | Technical credibility |
| Feature 5 | Deep Bass Stereo Sound | Emotional appeal |
| Package | with Wireless Charging Case | Added value |
| Variant | (Midnight Black) | Color differentiation |

## Category-Specific Character Limits

Amazon enforces these HARD. Exceed them and your listing gets suppressed.

| Category | Max Characters | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Electronics | 200 | Most strict |
| Home & Kitchen | 200 | Include dimensions here |
| Clothing | 150 | Include size in title |
| Beauty | 200 | Include volume/weight |
| Sports | 200 | Include material |
| Toys | 150 | Age range mandatory |
| Books | N/A | Follow KDP guidelines |
| All others | 200 | Default limit |

**Critical:** Mobile truncates titles at ~80 characters. Put your most important keywords in the first 80 characters.

## Keyword Placement: What Goes Where

Amazon's algorithm weights title keywords by position. Earlier = more weight.

```
Position 1-50 chars:  HIGHEST WEIGHT → Put your primary keyword here
Position 51-100 chars: MEDIUM WEIGHT → Put secondary keywords
Position 101-150 chars: LOWER WEIGHT → Feature keywords
Position 151-200 chars: LOWEST WEIGHT → Variant info
```

**This means:** "Wireless Earbuds, 40-Hour Battery..." ranks better than "40-Hour Battery Wireless Earbuds..." — because the core keyword "Wireless Earbuds" appears first.

## Capitalization Rules

Amazon's style guide:

✅ Correct:
- "Wireless Earbuds — 40-Hour Battery"
- First letter of each word capitalized (except prepositions under 5 letters)
- Numbers as digits (not words)

❌ Wrong:
- "WIRELESS EARBUDS" (all caps = suppressed)
- "wireless earbuds" (all lowercase = unprofessional)
- Every letter of every word capitalized (looks spammy)

## What NOT to Put in Your Title

Amazon will suppress your listing for:

- ❌ Price ("$29.99")
- ❌ Promotional language ("Best Seller", "Top Quality", "100% Guaranteed")
- ❌ ALL CAPS words
- ❌ Special characters (!!!, ★, ♛)
- ❌ Competitor brand names
- ❌ "Free Shipping", "On Sale"
- ❌ Seller name or contact info
- ❌ Subjective claims ("Amazing", "Incredible")

## The 5-Minute Title Audit

Run your existing title through these checks:

1. ☐ **Primary keyword in first 50 characters?**
2. ☐ **Under 200 characters total?**
3. ☐ **No all-caps? No special characters?**
4. ☐ **At least 3 features with specific numbers?**
5. ☐ **Brand name first?**

Score 5/5? Your title is solid. Score 3/5 or less? Rewrite it.

## FAQ

### How often should I update my title?
Only when you have new data. If rankings are dropping, check your title against new competitors. Otherwise, change it no more than once per quarter — frequent changes can trigger re-indexing.

### Should I include the brand name if I'm not brand registered?
Yes. It builds recognition over time. If you don't have a brand yet, create a simple, unique brand name (not a generic keyword).

### What if my product has too many features for 200 characters?
Prioritize. Pick the top 5 features that matter MOST to your target customer. Put the rest in bullet points and backend search terms.

### Do emojis help in Amazon titles?
No. Amazon strips emojis from titles. Don't use them.
